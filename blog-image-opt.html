WebP vs PNG: Optimizing Images for Core Web Vitals

Udit Sharma 3 Min Read

I audited a client's website recently. Their homepage was 4MB. 3.5MB of that was just images. This is an SEO killer. Google's Core Web Vitals penalize slow loading sites heavily.

Why PNG is destroying your speed

PNG is great for transparency, but it's a lossless format from the 90s. It keeps too much data that the human eye can't even see. A typical hero banner in PNG can easily be 1MB+.

Enter WebP (Google's Format)

WebP provides superior compression. I have seen 500KB PNGs turn into 40KB WebP images with zero visible loss in quality. That is a 90% reduction.

How to Implement?

You don't need manual tools. Use the <picture> tag to serve WebP to modern browsers and PNG to older ones as a fallback.
